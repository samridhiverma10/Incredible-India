If you're looking for a brush with divine, the “Ashtavinayak” temples in the state of Maharashtra dedicated to Lord Ganesh are the places to be. Devotees throng to these temples in large number because it is believed that a pilgrimage to the Ashtavinayak ensures the pilgrim everlasting bliss. Glorified as 'Swayambhu' or self-emanated, each of the eight idols have distinctive features. Pune, the second largest city of the state, can be used as a base to visit these shrines. These Swayambhu shrines, in order of importance are Morgaon, Siddhatek, Pali, Madh, Theur, Lenyadri, Ozar and Ranjangaon.
